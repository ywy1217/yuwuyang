Smart Diaper

An autonomous diaper-embedded sensing platform with on-board urine-activated battery for urinary tract disease screening

Urinary tract infections (UTIs) are the second most common infection in the body accounting for about 7 and one million office and emergency department visits, respectively, and 100,000 hospitalizations every year. Even though mostly UTIs are easily treatable with antibiotics, if not identified and treated timely, they can be a major cause of additional morbidity (ascending infection, loss of kidney function, and sepsis) in infants and elderly patients suffering from dementia (the two main groups using diapers).

The invented diaper-embedded system is an inexpensive autonomous urinary disease sensing platform with an on-board urine-activated battery, as an early screening alternative to conventional dipstick urinalysis which otherwise requires user intervention. The system is a thin and conformal add-on into a diaper, which autonomously wakes up upon a urination event, analyzes the urine and sends the detection results wirelessly to a remote receiver.

nitrate sensor

Low-cost nitrate sensor for precision agriculture application

As one of the demands of precision agriculture, accurate measurement of soil macronutrients (such as N, P and K) is important for adjusting fertilizer application rates spatially, improving agricultural production and preventing excessive use of fertilizers which otherwise causes (groundwater) contamination. Real-time, in-field and non-destructive detecting methods are desired in modern farm management to reduce operational expense and increase testing throughput and efficientcy.

Printed biodegradable nitrate sensor array with tunable working windows is developed as a low-cost monitoring tool.
